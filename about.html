about the artist

Creative Vision

My work explores the intersection of waste and beauty, transforming discarded materials into thought-provoking art pieces. Through sculpture, mixed media, and installation work, I challenge perceptions about value, consumption, and environmental responsibility.

Each piece tells a story of transformation - from forgotten objects to meaningful art. I work primarily with found materials, electronic waste, textiles, and natural elements, creating a dialogue between technology, nature, and human experience.

Process & Materials

My creative process begins on the streets, in landfills, and in the forgotten corners of our urban landscape. I collect materials that others have discarded, seeing potential where others see waste.

Electronic Waste

Circuit boards, smartphones, cables, and computer components

Industrial Materials

Metal fragments, factory remnants, gears, and mechanical parts

Textiles & Papers

Vintage clothing, maps, documents, and fabric scraps

Natural Elements

Living plants, moss, sea glass, and weathered wood

Artist Statement

"In a world that constantly creates and discards, I find my voice in the forgotten fragments. Every piece of electronic waste, every torn fabric, every rusted gear carries stories of human ambition, obsolescence, and resilience. My art doesn't just repurpose materials - it resurrects memories and reimagines futures."

"Through my work, I invite viewers to reconsider their relationship with consumption, technology, and the environment. What we throw away doesn't disappear - it transforms, and in that transformation lies both warning and hope."
